Something went wrong.

Byzantium Linux was unable to automatically configure itself.

Chances are, your computer has a wireless chipset that we don't have drivers for yet. This is a problem.

There are lots of things you could do to troubleshoot, but if this is an emergency chances are you don't have a lot of time. If you have a USB wi-fi dongle, plug it into this computer and reboot Byzantium Linux. We support lots of USB wireless devices so chances are our driver kit will detect and configure it. When you have time later please file a trouble ticket and we'll fix it in the next release.

Regardless, you have a fully functional desktop at your fingertips, so you can plug USB drives in, copy and edit files, and do other workstation things (just click the icon in the bottom-left corner). You can also plug mobile devices into this computer to recharge them.
